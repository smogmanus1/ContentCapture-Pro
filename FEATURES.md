# ContentCapture Pro

## *Save it once. Recall it forever. Share it anywhere.*

---

## The Problem

You find amazing content online every day — articles, recipes, tutorials, research, news. But then what?

- **Bookmarks pile up** and become a graveyard of links you never revisit
- **Copy-paste is tedious** — open the page, select, copy, switch apps, paste, repeat
- **Social sharing is painful** — character limits, formatting, switching between platforms
- **You lose track** of that perfect article you read last month

## The Solution

**ContentCapture Pro** transforms how you save, organize, and share web content.

Press **Ctrl+Alt+P** on any webpage. Give it a short name like "recipe" or "article". Done.

Now, anywhere you can type — Word, email, Facebook, Slack, anywhere — just type `::recipe::` and your content appears instantly.

### 🧠 Intelligent Search & Indexing

**"How do you remember 7,000 entries?"**

Easy. ContentCapture Pro doesn't just store your captures — it **indexes them** for lightning-fast retrieval.

#### The Problem With Human Memory

You saved an article about inflation three months ago. What did you name it?
- Was it "inflation"? "economy"? "fed-rates"? "news-march"?
- You remember *what* it was about, but not *what you called it*

#### The Solution: Search Everything

ContentCapture Pro searches across **all fields simultaneously**:

| Field | What It Searches |
|-------|------------------|
| **Name** | The hotstring name you created |
| **Title** | The original webpage title |
| **URL** | The full web address |
| **Tags** | Your categorization tags |
| **Notes** | Your personal notes |
| **Opinion** | Your commentary |
| **Body** | The full captured content |

**Type "inflation" in Quick Search** → Finds every capture that mentions inflation *anywhere* — in the title, the body, your notes, or the URL.

#### Speed That Scales

| Captures | Search Time |
|----------|-------------|
| 100 | Instant |
| 1,000 | Instant |
| 5,000 | Instant |
| 10,000+ | Still instant |

The secret? **Alphabetized indexing** and **in-memory search**. Your captures are loaded once at startup and searched in RAM — no slow disk reads, no database queries.

#### How It Works (For the Curious)

1. **On startup**: All captures load into a sorted Map structure
2. **Hotstring lookup**: O(1) hash table lookup — finds `::recipe::` in microseconds
3. **Search**: Parallel text matching across all fields
4. **Index file**: Maintains alphabetical order for fast browsing

**Your brain forgets. ContentCapture Pro doesn't.**

Type a few letters of what you *remember* — a word from the title, a tag you used, a phrase from the article — and it finds exactly what you need.

## 🚀 Getting Started

### Requirements
- Windows 10/11
- [AutoHotkey v2](https://www.autohotkey.com/) installed
- Any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Brave)

### Installation
1. Download ContentCapture Pro
2. Run `ContentCapture.ahk`
3. First launch opens the Setup wizard
4. You're ready to capture!

### Your First Capture
1. Go to any webpage
2. Press **Ctrl+Alt+P**
3. Name it something short (e.g., "test")
4. Open any text field and type `::test::`
5. Watch your content appear! 🎉

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Keep names short** — "pasta" is faster to type than "italian-pasta-recipe"

2. **Use the ? suffix** when you're not sure what you want — `::name?::` shows all options

3. **Save short versions** for social media — next time it's one-click sharing

4. **Star your favorites** — they appear in the tray menu for quick access

5. **Use tags** — filter by #news, #tutorial, #work to find things fast

6. **Set up AI** for summarizing long articles — game changer for research
