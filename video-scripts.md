## VIDEO 1: Installation (2-3 minutes)

### BEFORE RECORDING
- Open Windows Sandbox (or clean PC)
- Have ContentCapture-Pro folder ready to drag in
- Close unnecessary windows

---

### SCRIPT

**[INTRO - Show desktop]**

> "Installing ContentCapture Pro takes about one minute. Let me show you how."

**[Drag ContentCapture-Pro folder into Sandbox]**

> "First, download ContentCapture Pro from GitHub and unzip it."

**[Open the folder, show files]**

> "You'll see these files. The only one you need is install.bat."

**[Double-click install.bat]**

> "Double-click install.bat. That's it."

**[Installer opens - show the welcome screen]**

> "The installer checks if AutoHotkey is installed on your system."

**[If AHK not found - installer prompts to download]**

> "Since this is a fresh computer, it needs to download AutoHotkey version 2. 
> This is the engine that runs ContentCapture Pro. It's free and safe."

**[Press Y to install]**

> "Press Y for Yes."

**[AutoHotkey installer opens]**

> "The AutoHotkey installer opens. Just click through with the defaults."
> "Install... Next... Next... Finish."

**[Back to install.bat - launching ContentCapture Pro]**

> "Now the installer launches ContentCapture Pro."

**[Prompt about startup]**

> "It asks if you want it to start automatically when Windows starts.
> I recommend Yes - that way it's always ready when you need it."

**[Press Y]**

> "Press Y."

**[Installation complete screen]**

> "That's it! Look for the green H icon in your system tray down here."

**[Point to system tray area]**

> "That means ContentCapture Pro is running and ready."

**[OUTRO]**

> "Installation done. In the next video, I'll show you how to capture your first content."

## VIDEO 2: Your First Capture (3-4 minutes)

### BEFORE RECORDING
- Have a browser open with an interesting article
- Make sure ContentCapture Pro is running (green H in tray)

---

### SCRIPT

**[INTRO - Show browser with article]**

> "Let's capture your first piece of content. I'm on an article I want to save and share later."

**[Highlight some text on the page]**

> "First, I'll highlight the part I want to save. This could be a quote, a summary, 
> or the whole article - whatever you want."

**[Press Ctrl+Alt+C]**

> "Now press Control, Alt, C. That's your capture shortcut."

**[Capture dialog appears]**

> "The capture window pops up."

**[Point to each field]**

> "Let's look at what we have:"
>
> "Script Name - this is the shortcut you'll type. It auto-generates from the title,
> but you can change it to something easy to remember. Let's call this one... 'myarticle'."

**[Type a simple name]**

> "URL - already filled in from your browser."
>
> "Title - grabbed from the page."
>
> "Body - this is the text you highlighted."
>
> "You can also add tags to organize, and your own opinion or notes."

**[Click Save]**

> "Click Save."

**[TrayTip notification appears]**

> "Done! See that notification? Your content is saved."

**[Open Notepad or any text field]**

> "Now let's use it. Open any place you can type - email, Word, a text box, anywhere."

**[Type the script name]**

> "Type your script name... 'myarticle'..."

**[Content appears]**

> "Boom! Your content appears instantly."

**[Clear the text, demonstrate variations]**

> "But wait, there's more. Try these:"
>
> "Type 'myarticle' then 'go'..."

**[Type scriptname + go]**

> "It opens the original webpage!"

**[Type scriptname + c]**

> "Type 'myarticle' then 'c'... it copies to your clipboard without pasting."

**[Type scriptname + ?]**

> "Can't remember the commands? Type 'myarticle' then a question mark."

**[Help popup appears]**

> "This shows you ALL the available shortcuts for that content."

**[OUTRO]**

> "You just captured content and accessed it three different ways. 
> In the next video, I'll show you how to share directly to social media."

## GENERAL RECORDING TIPS

### Voice
- Speak slowly and clearly
- Pause after important actions
- It's okay to re-record sections

### Screen
- Use a clean desktop
- Zoom in on important areas (magnifier tool: Win + Plus)
- Hide personal bookmarks/files

### Editing
- Cut out mistakes
- Add text callouts for shortcuts (Ctrl+Alt+C)
- Add subtle zoom on mouse clicks

### Length
- Keep each video under 4 minutes
- People drop off after 3 minutes
- Make separate videos, not one long one

### Software Suggestions
- **Free:** OBS Studio, ShareX
- **Paid:** Camtasia, ScreenFlow (Mac)
- **Quick & Easy:** Windows built-in (Win+G for Game Bar)
